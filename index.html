I’m a history and culture nerd, and here’s my perspective. (5min read)



As a non-black, non-white race, it is perhaps the easiest to be unbiased and objective. Being an Asian foreigner in America, I was not an exception to racism. In fact, Asian Americans face racism not only from Whites, but also from Blacks and others. So I thought about this a lot growing up here.

Blacks were brought to America as slaves mostly from West Africa. Through slavery, blacks entirely lost their identity and culture, including the ‘Nigerian-variant’ of linguistic features, cuisine, and etc. The only ‘culture’ that was left was the western variant culture of being a slave, uneducated and poor. And as culture and wealth(or the lack of) flows generation by generation, this damaged culture stayed, resulting in problems of violence, poverty, broken families, etc till this day.

Similar can be said about nations that faced western colonialism, like Central/South America, Africa, etc. although their existing culture was not completely deleted like the case of African Americans.

If you or your family are from Asia, anywhere from Middle East, South Asia, SEA, East Asia, this might be hard to imagine. As long as our history is, our cultures are very intricate and complex, because we are collectivistic. This complexity is not present in European/Western culture. This is not a bad thing by any means by itself.

Analogy I’d like to make is 0 is a number that notates the lack of number. Comparing Asian cultures to the western culture is like comparing 1 to 0. They are both numbers, meaning they are both cultures, but one has centralized, universal sets of rules to follow, and the other does not. Western culture is “individualistic”, meaning the culture is individualized. Every cultural characteristics you see in the west exists purely through individuals, whereas in Asian cultures, there is an omnipresent energy that defines how you should live, act, love, eat, etc.

Western culture very often uses these cultural differences as they see it to morally judge other cultures with the excuse of human rights. "The people are not “free” in that country", etc. This is incredibly wrong and ignorant, and it is what fueled colonialism, but that’s another story.

There are exceptions though, especially in European nations adjacent to the Mediterranean Sea, where there were a lot of trades, influences, and annexations from Middle Eastern and Muslim cultures, i.e. Roman, Byzantine, Ottoman, Andalusian Spain, etc. Also, modern-day westernization that is happening across Asia did indeed dilute the distinct collectivist and individualistic cultures, especially for Asians living in the west.

Going back to the African American culture, because of slavery, their ‘history’ only started from slavery, as anything before was deleted. In other words, they really didn’t have enough time or a chance to fully define their identity and culture, outside of being a slave and poor. As much as we love a lot of aspects of the current African American culture, especially regarding pop-culture, music, etc, in a wholistic sense, it’s not life-sustainable, and is in need of recovery.

If we were to fault the blacks for not staying in school and resorting to violence, we should really fault the root, which is the culture that originated from slavery. Then the responsibility goes to the current government and the lack of systemic support to reeducate and lift up the black communities across America. So why isn’t the government doing it?

The problem is, western culture just doesn’t allow for a collectivistic perspective. Racism is a problem of an entire racial group(s), not individuals. To even recognize the existence of this problem, we need to be able to talk about the race from a big picture, not individuals to individuals.

White Americans do not recognize the problems of their own culture, because they think it is just ‘those’ individuals being racist. But as we compared, 0 is also a number. A culture that allows for high individual variance is at fault, if it allows racist act to go unbothered, and unpressured. In other words, “That’s none of my business” and “I don’t judge you, why do you judge me” mindset enables racism to survive and spread through generation by generation.

This “minding his/her own business” mindset keeps people in their own comfort zone. The most important thing in the West is doing what you want to do, and enjoying individualistic choices, preferences and ‘freedom’. Majority of White Americans in White areas don’t need to know or care about things that don’t affect them. As a result, they are ignorant of other cultures, others’ perspectives, history, and ethic/racial issues. This is also replicated in Western history books. “World History” really only deals with history that affected Europe/NA, either directly, or indirectly. History that did not contribute to the Western identity is treated as “niche” history.

Conclusion:

The negative aspects of the current African American culture is a result of western colonialism and racism. There is a current need for the modern-day western culture in America to accept collectivism, for the sake of minorities and to get rid of the immediate race problems in America, especially for African Americans, who never had the chance to define themselves outside of slavery. Obviously, this is both idealistic and unrealistic, as changing a culture takes money and power to control the media over a long period of time. But thankfully, it is slowly happening, because of minorities with collectivistic cultures like ourselves are making impact in all fields and aspects of the country.

We are all just products of culture(s). No human existence can escape it. Culture is the blueprint and formula in which we are produced out of. The variance in individuals is determined by the blueprint and the formula. High variance, like with the case of individualistic culture, allows individuals for innovation, great arts, etc, but also allows for terrible things, like colonialism, racism, and more. What do we value more?



TLDR: The root of racism is Western Culture and the solution is to dilute its individualistic nature with collectivism.



For those who want to challenge by saying “racism existed in other cultures outside of Europe/West too”, it’s definitely a discussion to be had. But the way I see it is, hate is hate, and are all equally bad, but every cultures had different hate. Europe had hate based on gender and race, Africa had tribes/family/ethnic identity, Middle East had religion (and race later on, due to Hellenic and Roman Influences), South Asian/Desi culture had ethnocentrism, and race, religion later on its history from Arab invasions and British colonialism, East Asia has ethnocentrism/nationalism and classism. (This is not an exclusive list)

Racism might exist in other parts of the world today due to westernization, but at its root, racism enacted by a white person and non-white person is fundamentally different. White’s racism is purely racial, whereas others’ racism is mixed with culture, religion, the perception of wealth, ethnic identity, nationality, premature stereotypes, historic animosity, etc. No other time in history no where else was there a unification of a race to conquer another race, as it happened in Europe against Africa.
