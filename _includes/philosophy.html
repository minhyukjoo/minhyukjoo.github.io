The Root of Racism

It is challenging to avoid the topic of racism while living in America, especially as a minority. Perhaps being an East Asian is the easiest way to be objective about it, considering that many East Asian countries practiced isolationism until the modern era. In layman’s terms, isolationism is the reason why East Asians weren’t “in the race war” until now.

This writing attempts to combine all of the knowledge I have on different ethnic groups, dissecting its cultural DNA, and objectively defining what is a race and what is a culture. This writing not only talks about racism, but also defines what the root cause is, and how to resolve it. But before considering if it is even humanly possible, should racism be solved? And at what cost?

Coexistence of Utopia and Dystopia

In philosophy, it’s not rare to come across terms like utopia and dystopia. Extreme-polar-ends terms like these are a fundamental logical makeup of the universe. But the more depth in reason you dig into, the more similar these two polar ends become. For example, if I were to call everyone I know as my best friends, are there any best friends left? Because everyone is my best friend, no one is. If everyone had an infinite amount of money, it is as if no one has any, also known as inflation. Just like how every departure is an arrival.

This writing explains why what seems utopic is actually dystopian, and vice versa, particularly using surveillance society as examples. What protects my privacy more? Locking up my information in a secured chest and hiding it under my bed? Or dumping my information in the ocean of information? This was my final essay for a philosophy class in college, but I like what I wrote so I'm sharing it here.

Philosophy isn’t a hobby you can easily express to people around you. But I am so passionate about it so I started writing it down, which seems like the only thing I can think of to actualize my passion for it. I was born atheist, but became Christian, ironically through secular philosophy.

Since when I was in middle school, it always bothered me that in history classes, who was a hero for one nation was a villain for another. I felt the need to discover an objective way to view the world. By the end of my high school years, I had fully developed my own mixture of nihilism, relativism, and existentialism without even knowing the meaning of those words nor the philosophers behind it. I knew that I had reached the end of reasoning with it, but I was severely disappointed. Because I found out that, in the secular world, what is the most objective is to be completely subjective.

My disappointment led to more reasoning. After researching all of the major religions and philosophies, both alive and dead, I landed on Christianity. I never looked back ever since. I am a “secular-existentialist-turned-Christian-apologist”, objectively defending my Christian faith in the most secular way.
